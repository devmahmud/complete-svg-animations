# Advanced Path Techniques

<Objective>
- Use `<clipPath>` to mask SVG content with arbitrary shapes
- Combine animated paths with `<mask>` for reveal effects
- Apply gradients and filters via `<defs>` references
</Objective>

These techniques combine paths with `<defs>`, `<clipPath>`, `<mask>`, and gradients — they're how production-grade SVG illustrations are built.

## Clipping with a path

A `<clipPath>` defines a shape that **constrains visibility**. Anything outside the clip is invisible.

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <defs>
    <clipPath id="dot">
      <circle cx="50" cy="50" r="40"/>
    </clipPath>
  </defs>
  <!-- A wide gradient strip clipped to a circle -->
  <rect x="-100" y="0" width="300" height="100"
        fill="url(#stripeGrad)" clip-path="url(#dot)">
    <animate attributeName="x" values="-100;0" dur="3s" repeatCount="indefinite"/>
  </rect>
  <defs>
    <linearGradient id="stripeGrad" x1="0%" x2="100%">
      <stop offset="0%" stop-color="#7c5cff"/>
      <stop offset="50%" stop-color="#ffd43b"/>
      <stop offset="100%" stop-color="#3ddc97"/>
    </linearGradient>
  </defs>
</svg>`} caption="The rect slides; only the circular hole is visible. The clip itself is a path." />

## Masks: paint with the alpha channel

A `<mask>` is like a clip, but it uses **luminance** — white means "show," black means "hide," gray means partial transparency. Combine masks with stroke animation for reveal effects:

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <defs>
    <mask id="reveal">
      <rect width="100" height="100" fill="black"/>
      <path d="M 10 50 L 90 50" stroke="white" stroke-width="30"
            stroke-linecap="round" stroke-dasharray="100" stroke-dashoffset="100">
        <animate attributeName="stroke-dashoffset" values="100;0;-100"
                 dur="3s" repeatCount="indefinite"/>
      </path>
    </mask>
  </defs>
  <rect width="100" height="100" fill="#7c5cff" mask="url(#reveal)"/>
</svg>`} caption="The mask's path strokes left-to-right, revealing the violet rect." />

## Gradients along a path

Gradients are defined as fills/strokes; you reference them by id. The gradient's coordinate system follows the bounding box of the shape it fills.

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <defs>
    <linearGradient id="ribbon" x1="0%" x2="100%">
      <stop offset="0%" stop-color="#7c5cff"/>
      <stop offset="50%" stop-color="#ffd43b"/>
      <stop offset="100%" stop-color="#3ddc97"/>
    </linearGradient>
  </defs>
  <path d="M 10 20 Q 50 10 90 20 Q 50 30 10 20"
        fill="url(#ribbon)" stroke="white" stroke-width="0.5">
    <animate attributeName="d"
             values="M 10 20 Q 50 10 90 20 Q 50 30 10 20;
                     M 10 80 Q 50 70 90 80 Q 50 90 10 80;
                     M 10 20 Q 50 10 90 20 Q 50 30 10 20"
             dur="3s" repeatCount="indefinite"/>
  </path>
</svg>`} caption="Morphing path + linearGradient fill = a moving rainbow ribbon." />

## Filters: glow with `<filter>`

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <defs>
    <filter id="glow" x="-50%" y="-50%" width="200%" height="200%">
      <feGaussianBlur in="SourceGraphic" stdDeviation="2"/>
      <feMerge>
        <feMergeNode/>
        <feMergeNode in="SourceGraphic"/>
      </feMerge>
    </filter>
  </defs>
  <circle cx="50" cy="50" r="20" fill="#ffd43b" filter="url(#glow)">
    <animate attributeName="r" values="18;26;18" dur="2s" repeatCount="indefinite"/>
  </circle>
</svg>`} caption="A blurred copy is merged behind the original, giving a glow halo." />

<Callout type="tip" title="Define once, reuse everywhere">
  <code>&lt;defs&gt;</code> holds reusable bits (gradients, filters, paths). Reference by <code>url(#id)</code> as many times as you want — the renderer caches them.
</Callout>
