# Coordinate System

<Objective>
- Read SVG coordinates correctly (origin at top-left, y grows down)
- Understand `viewBox` as a virtual camera that pans and zooms
- Make any SVG responsive at any rendered size
</Objective>

## Understanding Coordinates

SVGs use a **coordinate system** where:

- Origin (0,0) is at the **top-left corner**
- X-axis increases **rightward**
- Y-axis increases **downward** (the opposite of math class)
- Units are **user units**, not pixels — they're scaled by the viewBox

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <!-- 0,0 reference dot -->
  <circle cx="0" cy="0" r="2" fill="#ff5d73"/>
  <text x="4" y="9" font-size="6" fill="#ff5d73">0,0</text>

  <!-- A point at 70,70 -->
  <circle cx="70" cy="70" r="2" fill="#ffd43b"/>
  <text x="73" y="74" font-size="6" fill="#ffd43b">70,70</text>

  <!-- Axis arrows -->
  <line x1="0" y1="0" x2="40" y2="0" stroke="#7c5cff" stroke-width="0.5"/>
  <line x1="0" y1="0" x2="0" y2="40" stroke="#7c5cff" stroke-width="0.5"/>
</svg>`} caption="Move the 70,70 dot. Notice y grows downward." />

## ViewBox — the SVG camera

The `viewBox` attribute defines the **window** the renderer uses to look at the canvas. Anything outside the window is clipped.

```html
<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <!-- Shows a 100×100 area starting at (0,0) -->
</svg>

<svg viewBox="50 50 100 100">
  <!-- Shows a 100×100 area starting at (50,50) -->
</svg>
```

**Syntax:** `viewBox="x y width height"` — the first two values pan the camera, the second two zoom it.

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <rect x="0" y="0" width="100" height="100" fill="rgba(124,92,255,0.08)"/>
  <rect x="30" y="30" width="40" height="40" fill="#7c5cff"/>
</svg>`} caption='Try changing viewBox to "30 30 40 40" — the inner square fills the frame.' />

## Responsive SVGs

ViewBox makes SVGs scale to any rendered size while preserving proportions:

```html
<svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="16" height="16">…</svg>
<svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="128" height="128">…</svg>
```

Same source, different sizes — pixel-perfect at any scale.

<Callout type="tip" title="ViewBox is your design canvas">
  Pick a viewBox once (24×24 for icons, 100×100 for examples, 800×600 for illustrations) and place every shape in those units. The browser handles the pixel math.
</Callout>
