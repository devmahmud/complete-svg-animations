# How Shapes Are Made

<Objective>
- Distinguish **fill** (interior) from **stroke** (outline) and apply both
- Compose complex graphics from simple primitives
- Use **source order** to control which shape paints on top
</Objective>

When you draw on paper you sketch the outline, then either color inside or leave it as a line. SVG works the same way — every shape is a path with two paint properties: **fill** (interior color) and **stroke** (outline color + thickness).

## Outline vs filled

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 200 100">
  <!-- Just the outline -->
  <rect x="20" y="30" width="60" height="40" fill="none" stroke="#7c5cff" stroke-width="3"/>

  <!-- Filled shape -->
  <rect x="120" y="30" width="60" height="40" fill="#7c5cff" stroke="#ffd43b" stroke-width="3"/>
</svg>`} caption="Same path, different paint. Try setting fill='none' on the right one." />

The order is meaningful: SVG draws the **fill first, stroke on top**, so a thick stroke actually overlaps the fill region by half its width.

## Composition: build big from small

Complex icons are stacks of simple shapes. Here's a monitor:

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <!-- Screen -->
  <rect x="20" y="20" width="60" height="40" rx="3" fill="none" stroke="#ede9de" stroke-width="3"/>
  <!-- Stand -->
  <line x1="50" y1="60" x2="50" y2="76" stroke="#ede9de" stroke-width="3"/>
  <!-- Base -->
  <line x1="35" y1="80" x2="65" y2="80" stroke="#ede9de" stroke-width="3" stroke-linecap="round"/>
</svg>`} caption="Three primitives, one icon. Reorder them — z-index follows source order." />

## Layering: source order = paint order

The element that appears **later in the markup paints on top**. There's no `z-index` in SVG; you reorder by moving lines.

<LiveSVG code={`<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <circle cx="40" cy="50" r="22" fill="#7c5cff"/>
  <circle cx="60" cy="50" r="22" fill="#ffd43b"/>
</svg>`} caption="Swap the two circles to put violet on top." />

</svg>`} caption="Order matters — the largest must come first so the smaller ones paint on top." />
</TryIt>

<Callout type="tip" title="Mental model">
  An SVG is a stack of painted paths. To debug a missing shape, ask: is its <code>fill</code> set? Is something else painting over it? Is it inside a clip?
</Callout>
